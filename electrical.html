Electrical Team's Quarterly Milestones

Q1 Milestones

MPPT Voltage Regulation & Irradiance Simulation

We validated the feasibility of our power distribution system by simulating solar irradiance using a bench power supply (12V–20V). The MPPT charge controller successfully maintained battery output voltage within a tight ±2% tolerance (11.9V to 12.3V). This test confirmed that our selected regulation hardware can provide stable power to the MCU and sensors even under rapid fluctuations caused by shading or clouds.

Power Conversion Efficiency Audit

An efficiency audit was performed across the system’s three voltage rails. While the DC-DC stages (Buck and Boost) performed at an exceptional 93% and 94.5% efficiency respectively, the DC-AC inverter was identified as a bottleneck at 65.5% efficiency. This analysis provided the "green light" for our DC-only pathway (22.06% total efficiency) while highlighting the need for a higher-efficiency inverter in the next design phase.

Battery Capacity & Solar Yield Modeling

We finalized the energy storage architecture centered on a 12.8V 50Ah LiFePO4 battery, providing 640 Wh of total storage. Theoretical modeling of a 100W panel indicated a daily yield of 210–337 Wh, which is sufficient to cover the system's control electronics (50–120 Wh/day) while leaving a surplus for external device charging. This confirms our power budget is well-balanced for the project's intended use-case.
