SolUmbra

Introduction

SolUmbra is a portable, human-scale microgrid designed to provide reliable, renewable energy in environments where the grid is either unavailable or untrustworthy. The system integrates a ruggedized wheeled cart with a semi-autonomous deployable solar array and an onboard energy storage subsystem.

Our mission at Charge4ward is to bridge the gap between renewable energy and remote accessibility. SolUmbra is not just a charging station; it is a rapid-deployment power solution built for the people who need it most—from disaster relief teams and medical shelters to NGOs and community response groups. By prioritizing safety and ease of use for non-technical users, we ensure that clean power is available at the push of a button, anywhere the sun shines.

Problem Statement

The Challenge of Energy Accessibility

Many global locations lack reliable access to clean electrical power, forcing a dependency on fuel-based generators that are loud, high-maintenance, and environmentally harmful. With the Emergency Relief Solar Kit market hitting $1.23 billion in 2024, there is a massive demand for sustainable energy in disaster response, refugee support, and remote electrification.

The engineering challenge lies in creating a system that is both portable and resilient enough for field use. Current solutions often lack the durability for harsh environments or the efficiency to provide dependable electricity without constant manual adjustment.

Our Solution

By using solar power and integrated onboard storage, SolUmbra delivers dependable electricity without relying on grid access or fuel. Our design follows three core concepts:

  • Energy Management: Ability to input, filter, and output usable solar energy efficiently.
  • Durability: Ruggedized construction to make the system deployable in harsher, outdoor environments.
  • Intelligence: Semi-autonomous operation, adjusting the array position for optimal sunlight capture throughout the day.
